Electrical Power Planning of a Newly Developed Area

Grade: A+

Supervisor: Dr. Mohamed Hassan Soliman

Committee Members: Dr. Farouk Alhakeem, Eng. Tarek Abdelhameed

Project Scope

The project focused on designing the complete electrical infrastructure of a new city, including low voltage distribution systems for key buildings such as a residential area, a school, and a hotel.

My Contributions

  • Designed the power distribution system for a residential building, including panel schedules, circuit layouts, and cable routing using AutoCAD.
  • Designed and planned the CCTV system layout for a school, selecting camera types, coverage zones, and the NVR system.
  • Worked on the design of the fire alarm system for a hotel, ensuring proper detector placement, zoning, and compliance with safety codes.
  • Utilized AutoCAD to develop accurate and coordinated drawings for all three systems across the different buildings.

Key Learnings

  • Deepened my understanding of electrical distribution in residential projects, including load estimation, panel sizing, and proper circuit organization.
  • Gained practical experience in low-current systems—especially in CCTV and fire alarm design—enhancing my skills in system layout, device selection, and functional zoning.
  • Strengthened my proficiency in AutoCAD by working on real building plans, ensuring clarity, accuracy, and coordination with architectural layouts.
  • Developed problem-solving and system integration skills by designing systems that meet both technical standards and real-world constraints.
  • Enhanced my teamwork and communication abilities by collaborating with my team to align on design goals, resolve challenges, and deliver a cohesive project.
